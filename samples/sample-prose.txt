Rain moved through the city in long silver bands, washing the market lights into
soft halos. Tessa stood beneath the awning and read the letter again, tracing the
indent where the pen had pressed too hard on her name. It said to meet at dawn by
the river gate, and it carried no signature, only a faint smell of cedar.

She folded the page and tucked it into her sleeve. If she went, she might finally
learn why her brother vanished three winters ago. If she stayed, she would never
have to hear the truth.
